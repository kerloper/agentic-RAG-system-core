## Know Your Customer (KYC) Policy
Kerloper requires all customers to complete identity verification before they can access financial services. This process ensures that every account is associated with a verified individual or entity. KYC checks are designed to protect the platform from fraud and unauthorized use. The policy applies to both new and existing customers as part of ongoing risk management.

## Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy
Kerloper maintains a formal AML policy that monitors financial activity for suspicious behavior. The policy includes transaction screening and risk scoring based on customer profiles. Suspicious activity is escalated to the compliance team for further review. The objective is to detect and prevent funds derived from illicit sources.

## Data Retention Policy
Kerloper stores customer records and transaction history for the period required by regulation. Data is retained in a secure environment and is only kept as long as necessary for compliance and business purposes. The retention policy specifies review intervals and secure disposal methods. Retained information may include verification details, transaction logs, and account activity.

## Regulatory Framework
Kerloper operates under the regulatory framework that governs financial services, digital asset management, and payment facilitation. The platform complies with applicable laws in its primary operating jurisdiction. This includes requirements for customer protection, anti-fraud measures, and financial reporting. Compliance with this framework is a core part of Kerloper’s operational design.

## Compliance Violations Handling
Kerloper investigates any compliance violations through a dedicated compliance review process. Confirmed violations lead to remediation actions, including account restrictions or suspension. The platform also reports serious incidents to the appropriate regulators. Customers are notified of the findings and any follow-up steps required.
